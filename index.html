01

Colour Palette

Four colours make up the entire Attention Culture palette. Hot Pink is the brand's primary accent and should command attention. Black and White form the foundation of all communications. Grey provides a neutral surface for supporting content.

Hot Pink #EA124B Primary accent — hero moments, CTAs, highlights
Black #191919 Primary background — dark environments
Grey #EAEFEF Supporting surface — light environments
White #FFFFFF Primary background — light environments, body text on dark

Use Hot Pink sparingly

Hot Pink is reserved for moments that demand attention — CTAs, key data points, highlight text backgrounds, and key brand touchpoints. Overuse diminishes its impact.

Default to Black + White

Most brand communications live in Black and White. This creates a clean, high-contrast foundation that makes the Pink accent hit harder when it appears.

Grey for breathing room

Grey (#EAEFEF) is used as an alternative light surface — particularly in documents, presentations, and sections that need separation without reaching for White.

Never use colour gradients

All colours are used flat and solid. No gradients, tints, or transparency overlays of brand colours. Opacity is only ever applied to text, not background fills.

02

Logo

The Attention Culture logo exists in two forms: the stacked mark (ATTN / CUL• / TURE) and the full horizontal wordmark. Both are available in Black and White versions only. The logo is set in Shrimp — a typeface used exclusively for logo construction and never for any other brand communication.

Stacked Mark — White Use on black or dark backgrounds
Stacked Mark — Black Use on white or light backgrounds
Horizontal Wordmark — White Use on black or dark backgrounds
Horizontal Wordmark — Black Use on white or grey backgrounds
Logo on Pink

The White version of either logo mark may be placed on a Hot Pink background. This is an approved usage for high-impact brand moments.

Clear Space

Always maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the bullet point (•) in the stacked mark. Nothing enters this zone.

Never Do

Do not recolour the logo. Do not stretch or distort. Do not place on busy photographic backgrounds. Do not recreate using any typeface other than Shrimp.

04

Tone of Voice

Attention Culture is a challenger agency with a conscience. The brand voice is direct, purposeful, and culturally sharp — never corporate, never preachy. We speak with authority because we know this space, and we care about getting it right.

Direct

We say what we mean. No filler, no waffle. Every sentence earns its place. Short paragraphs, concrete claims, and clear next steps. Readers are busy — respect that.

Purposeful

Everything we communicate connects back to the mission: conscious brand experiences. We don't just describe what we do — we articulate why it matters for brands, audiences, and culture.

Culturally Sharp

We are embedded in gaming, music, and entertainment culture. We reference it authentically, never as an outsider trying to sound relevant. Jargon is earned, not performed.

Confident, Not Arrogant

We hold strong positions on how brands should engage with younger audiences. We share those positions clearly, but we stay curious and collaborative — not dismissive of other views.

We sound like this
  • Brands have a moral obligation to market consciously.
  • Gen Z aren't abandoning digital — they're rejecting algorithms.
  • Smaller communities. Deeper engagement. Better ROI.
  • Attention is human. It's sacred. Treat it that way.
  • Culture isn't a space to exploit — it's a space to protect.
Not like this
  • We leverage best-in-class synergies to optimise engagement metrics.
  • As a forward-thinking agency, we are passionate about our clients' success.
  • Touch base, circle back, move the needle, blue-sky thinking.
  • We're obsessed with disrupting the status quo! 🚀
  • At the end of the day, it's all about creating value for stakeholders.
05

Usage Examples

The brand system works across three primary environment types. Each has its own background and text combination — all drawn from the four-colour palette.

Dark Environment

Black background with white body copy and hot pink accents. The primary brand environment — used in decks, hero sections, reports, and digital experiences.

bg: #191919 — text: #FFFFFF

Light Environment

White background with black body copy. Used for document interiors, supporting slide layouts, and printed materials.

bg: #FFFFFF — text: #191919

Grey Environment

Grey background with black body copy. Used for supporting sections within documents and slide decks to create visual hierarchy without switching to full white.

bg: #EAEFEF — text: #191919

Pink Environment

Hot Pink backgrounds are reserved for high-impact brand moments — cover pages, key data callouts, CTAs, section dividers. Used at full saturation only. White logo and white text exclusively.

bg: #EA124B — text: #FFFFFF — logo: white variant only
